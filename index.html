DANGR: Domain-Adversarial Noise Generation for Robust Classification

Master's Thesis
Designed a domain generalization algorithm that injects adversarial noise into input/feature space to discourage reliance on domain-specific surface patterns (backgrounds, textures, styles). The classifier is forced to learn representations invariant across domains. Validated on PACS, VLCS, Office-Home benchmarks via DomainBed with systematic hyperparameter sweeps, demonstrating more stable generalization on unseen domains compared to baselines.
Domain Generalization DomainBed Adversarial Training PyTorch

Korean Anthropometry — Factor Analysis vs. PCA Comparison

Research Project
Compared FA and PCA on the 7th Korean Anthropometric Survey (n=6,380, 8 body-measurement variables). FA with Oblimin rotation revealed weight and length latent factors, though CFA fit was limited (CFI=.84, RMSEA=.299). PCA retained 90.2% of variance in 2 components and preserved strong predictive performance in gender classification (Logistic 87.5%, DT 85.2%, RF 87.8%, XGBoost 88.3%).
